Exponential Aging Deadline Scheduling

A Dynamic CPU Scheduling Algorithm

Introduction

Welcome to EAD Scheduling, a dynamic CPU scheduling algorithm that prioritizes processes using exponential aging deadlines.

Exponential Aging Deadline (EAD) Scheduling is a dynamic and adaptive scheduling algorithm designed to efficiently manage tasks with varying deadlines. It combines deadline proximity and waiting time to assign priorities, making it particularly useful in real-time systems where meeting deadlines is crucial. The priority of each task is calculated by incorporating both its deadline and the exponential aging factor of its waiting time, ensuring that processes nearing their deadlines are given higher priority. As tasks wait longer, their priority increases exponentially, reducing the risk of starvation and ensuring that no task is neglected for too long. EAD scheduling is ideal for environments with mixed workloads, such as multimedia streaming, real-time processing, and interactive user interfaces, where tasks with tight deadlines need to be handled promptly without sacrificing fairness. This approach guarantees that time-sensitive tasks are prioritized, improving system responsiveness and throughput while maintaining overall system efficiency.
